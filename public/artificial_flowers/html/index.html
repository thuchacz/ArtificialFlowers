Way for melting silk gum, or other- wise said tragacanth gum; One takes for this one patar of the said gum; it is necessary to put it to soak, in a half pint of rainwater, better more than less. One needs to leave it like that for two days, then one needs to take a spoon and stir it well, and hit it with it until such time it becomes a clear glue-pap, and if one sees that the soup is too thick, one needs to pour in a bit of water, until such time that it is good; then one needs to make it boil, a bit, and that while stirring it still. After that, one needs to pass it through a cloth, and after that, use it to starch silk with it. Way to make silk flowers Note: when the silk is very well peniez, put it on top of glass, very flat, the size of one window pane, reasonably big enough. Next, one needs to take the flat side of a knife, and take the silk gum with it. Next, one needs to spread it on top of the silk with it, and that also for a long time, that the silk becomes very well moistened, and fatty. After that, one needs to turn it over, and one needs to do the same. After that, one needs to put it on top of a towel, to let it dry in the wind, and or otherwise also. Next one needs to apply it, and put it on top of the form; and go over it with a pierre liquoire. And dry it near to the fire, and or else above a stove. Next, remove it from there. And then cut flowers from it, of all kinds that you want to make. But one needs always to keep silk prepared for making flowers between white paper. The same also for silk that is not yet prepared for making flowers. Way to undye cambric dyed with gamboge One needs to tie up the piece of cambric [to a rod?] quasi all as large as the piece and, next to wet it a bit, and rub it between the hands, well enough, then one needs to plunge it and wash it in hot water, or else in lye, like one washes white cambric, changing the water from time to time, until such that the cambric becomes perfectly white. One needs to tie up the piece of cambric two or three times for this end. One must boil that together with some small pieces of crimson fabric, which you all let boil until such that the dye becomes boiled out of the fabric and it becomes all undyed. Next one needs to take it out of the earthenware pot, which you keep for this use, and after having pressed the lye out, one + needs to take yet an other piece of incarlacque and make it boil in the same manner. But each time that you press the lye out, one needs always to re-cast it into the pot. One needs to continue like that all until such that you see that the incarlacque does not undye further and that it remains beautiful.
