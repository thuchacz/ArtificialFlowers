For making more pansy; for making alimon, and ranunculus; one needs to take, three mussel shells full of mien rouge [my red? Same red?], one needs to rub it well with a piece of marble, on top of some marble or some gres [sandstone?]; being well rubbed; one needs to throw it in a glass, or the third part of a glass, of the hide glue, with a mussel shell full of vermillion, one needs to stir it well, and to mix it together; next on needs to throw the white cambric into the glue, and turn it well, and press it well, and return it in the hands, rubbing it a little, in order that the dye will be well penetrated, and that the cambric, muslin; and toile will be a good red; after that, one needs to squeeze the glue, and dye within, so well that it stays reasonably well wetted, then one needs to hang in the attic; and being dry, the toile, cambric, and muslin; being cut into leaves, one needs to put a clear carmine on it; that you see to be a very beautiful color to your liking;
