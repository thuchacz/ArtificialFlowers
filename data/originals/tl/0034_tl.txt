Way to dye velum, or parchment,
for carnations, one puts in a little dish,
five, or six drops of gamboge, very clear,
like for stems, of the said flowers, next
one pours on top while stirring, the green from the
bottle, half, or the three parts of a
dish, that done, one takes a little piece
of sponge, one wets the end of it in this soup,
and one passes it over the velum, rather
thinly, then one leaves it to dry, next one
greens it again once more, and that, three, or four
times, in succession, that done, and being
very dry, one turns it on its other side,
and one does the same thing, if one sees after
that, that the velum, is green enough, one leaves it
to dry again well, next one plunges a
piece of sponge, very clean, in some egg
white, well beaten, and one passes over the velum
rather thinly also, but one needs to do one's [best as]
possible, that the velum, is everywhere, very
wet, being well dried, one turns it
