Behold the way that one needs to prepare it;
one needs to take a half quarter [of a pound]; or an ounce,
of verdigris, it must be well pounded, then
pass it through a thick little sieve; then one needs to rub it,
with some clear gum, and a few drops of vinegar;
on top of a marble slab, or of sandstone; being rubbed
enough; one needs to put it in a little pot, and thin
it with some vinegar; and from time, to time, stretch it
always like that; putting in some times some
drops of gum, without ever putting water in it;
because vinegar is the best
after that you use it with the paintbrush, for tracing
on paper, etc. ~~~
But when you want to have the green a little more
brown, one needs to put there a little more of the gum; ~
