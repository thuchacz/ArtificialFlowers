Note, red paper, is made again,
in this way; one takes a packet of
vermillion, or the amount of a mussel
shell,; with two mussel shells, of
minium, this is a coarse red dyestuff;
that is why, one needs to rub it well
on the marble; before mixing it
with the vermillion; after that one needs to take
clear gum, and vermillion, and 
minium, being mixed together in a dish, one
needs to pour a little of this clear gum
on top, and one mixes well together the two
dyes with a little stick, the dye being
well mixed; one needs to pour in the dye a
half glass of water, one needs to stir it all well
together; and after one needs to take a =
note without gum, water only [suffices] for 
making paper
