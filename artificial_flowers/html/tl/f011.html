The brown-blue and pale blue for archbishops, once cut, one needs to trace them with carmine. Another blue that one uses for the underside of archbishops, which one sees hardly at all because the colors are not worth anything even though they are beautiful in appearance. One needs to take a square or two of lacmus that one pounds, well bound in a good cloth, then put it to soak for one day and one night. Next, you boil it with a pint and a half of rainwater, better too little than too much. Pressing the cloth with a stick where it is blue, and inside, so that all of the blue leaves it. Then one needs to put the cambric or the cloths in the soup to boil together. Being boiled a little, one needs to take it out of the soup and hang it over cords. Being dry, if they are plainly not blue enough, one can plunge them again in the same soup, and that several times if there is need. Being dry, one puts them in fish glue or hide glue.
