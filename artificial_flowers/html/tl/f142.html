Way to dye paper red Put an ounce of potash in an earthenware pot, then boil a stoop of rainwater and pour it, all boiling, on the potash, let it stand untouched until the next day. Then gently, without stirring, pour the clear lye out into another clean pot. Then take a kanne of this lye, set it to boil and take a pound of red cloth put this in a clean pot and once the lye has boiled, pour it on the cloth, let it somewhat trecken on the fire, turning the cloth from time to time. Once it is all well drunken through, take remove it [from the fire] and let it stand to cool some then put the other kanne of lye on the fire. Let it boil. Then squeeze out the cloth as tightly as you can, and while the lye boils, put the cloth in it. Turn and stir it well. Let [it] boil for a quarter of an hour. Then let it cool a bit. Then put a bit of white tartar in a pot and pour the boiling dye on top of it. If you see that there is still foam forming, take it off, and and do not put it with the other foam.
