How one needs to accommodate cloths to make flowers After cambrics, muslins, and cloths are very well washed and well dried, one needs to take, or make, a nice starch fairly thick, and then starch your canvases or cambrics in the same manner, just as one starches coiffures. Then one needs to hang the cloth by cords. Being well dry, one needs to pass the hand over it, on both sides of the cloth, to render it very uniform, then fold them by hand; from there, put them in a paper. One needs to keep oneself from using the iron, as one [commonly] passes over cloths, nor other instruments for this end. One needs to observe this command for all other colors too. This sort of canvas can only serve for completely white flowers without being drawn upon.
