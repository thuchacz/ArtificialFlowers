Way to undye cambric dyed with gamboge One needs to tie up the piece of cambric [to a rod?] quasi all as large as the piece and, next to wet it a bit, and rub it between the hands, well enough, then one needs to plunge it and wash it in hot water, or else in lye, like one washes white cambric, changing the water from time to time, until such that the cambric becomes perfectly white. One needs to tie up the piece of cambric two or three times for this end. Cambric, dyed with saffron and gamboge, is undyed in the same manner also The way to undye crimson or incarlacque fabric One needs to take two pints of lye, made from vine ashes, with a half pint of rainwater or fountain water. One must boil that together with some small pieces of crimson fabric, which you all let boil until such that the dye becomes boiled out of the fabric and it becomes all undyed. Next one needs to take it out of the earthenware pot, which you keep for this use, and after having pressed the lye out, one + needs to take yet an other piece of incarlacque and make it boil in the same manner. But each time that you press the lye out, one needs always to re-cast it into the pot. One needs to continue like that all until such that you see that the incarlacque does not undye further and that it remains beautiful.
