But then let a pack of little cords dry and continue thus until that is red enough, according to your taste, all over up to six or seven times. [If] you want to have it shiny, go over it with egg white, finely beaten. This soup is also good to make silk red, especially a white ground or yellow. But [it] must first be washed clean af linten[?bits of the brazilwood?]. But one must cook herein a little bit. It is also good for making withe wood red. And once it is red enough, paint it with [egg] wash. It shines and is like mahogany. The ground of this can serve more than once. With other water and a bit more wood therein and alum, [it] is a good sap again. But the size of the alum [needed for] such a double bucket one surely learns oneself.
